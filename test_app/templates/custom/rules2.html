2. Learning Phase
During your learning phase, you may play any of the cards in your hand to make Wisdom. Ignore their normal costs and effects.
Each card generates Wisdom equal to its Wisdom value (on the left, in a dark box), if it has one. Otherwise it makes none.
You may learn any card from the supply by paying its Wisdom cost. Put newly learned cards in a separate pile to your discarded cards.

After you're done making Wisdom and learning things, discard your played cards and go to the end phase.

3. End Phase
If the supply has fewer than seven cards in it, add new ones from the supply deck until it's back to seven.
Optionally, you may 'prepare' one card from your hand by putting it back on top of your deck.
Discard the rest of your hand. Draw five new cards from your deck.

If at any point you try to draw from an empty deck, shuffle your discard pile and learned pile together and turn them over to form a new deck. Before doing this, for each card in your learning pile, trash a card from your discard pile. Set it aside; it's no longer in your deck.
